⚑ Deceased Discovered Mid-Call

Database showed the owner alive. A relative just revealed a death. This is the pivot.
Recognition cues (any one)
“he/she passed” • “we lost him/her” • “my late father/mother” • “since he died” • “the estate” • “she’s no longer with us” • “he’s been gone since…”
First, the tone
Someone may have just been reminded of a loss. Slow down. Condolence first, opportunity second. Do not sound excited that they died.
The pivot — say this
I’m so sorry for your loss, and I appreciate you telling me. This actually changes things in your favor. Because [SUBJECT] has passed, the funds don’t go away — they pass to the rightful heirs, and you may be entitled to claim them. Let me ask you a few things about the family so I can tell you exactly where you stand.
Then, in this order
1. Flip the claim type to Deceased: Undet. at the top of the screen.
2. Move to QUALIFY and begin establishing where they stand.
Routing the fork (only if it surfaces naturally)
You usually will not know which fork on the first call, and that is fine. Undetermined is the correct default. If it comes up on its own: was there a will, and did the estate go through probate?
Will and already probated → Probated.  •  No will → Intestate.  •  Unknown → stay Undetermined; Client Services confirms after the contract.
Entitlement caution — do not skip
The person on the phone is not automatically the claimant. Entitlement runs through heirship and there may be other heirs. Do not promise the money to this person. Establish heirship first.
Do not work out heirship or entitlement on the call. Client Services confirms it after the signed contract comes in.
Blended family, predeceased heirs with their own children, paternity questions, incomplete probate, or feuding family → escalate to supervisor. Do not resolve it on the call.
